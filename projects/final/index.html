Travel Trends

An exploration of trends in travel over the years and why certain destinations are hot spots.

By Samantha Cohen

International travel trends since Covid

“The worst year in tourism history.” It’s no surprise that 2020 was awarded this title. According to UN Tourism, before Covid-19, international tourist arrivals in 2019 were 1.5 billion. Once Covid hit, the World Health Organization deemed the outbreak, Covid-19, a global health emergency, then a pandemic. All worldwide destinations imposed travel restrictions while 27% kept their borders closed for international travel.

Between 2019 and 2020, international tourist arrivals declined about 75%, from 1.5 billion in 2019 to 381 million in 2020. This brought tourism back to levels seen during the 1990s.

Statista reports that international tourist arrivals increased by only 13 percent in 2021, and continued to recover as the years went on, reaching 88 percent of pre-pandemic levels in 2023.

In 2024, international tourism was reported to almost completely recover, at 99 percent, to pre-pandemic levels including several travel destinations surpassing 2019, pre-pandemic levels, according to UN Tourism.

Around 1.4 billion international tourists, which is classified as overnight visitors, were recorded internationally in 2024 which is an 11% increase compared to 2023, or 140 million more.

Most visited countries in the world

France, Spain and the United States are consistently at the top of the charts for welcoming the most international visitors year after year.

France:

Of course known for its famous Parisian tourist attractions like the Eiffel Tower or the Louvre Museum, France has centuries of history to explore, beautiful villages and nature to see and delicious pastries to enjoy. Home to the 2024 Olympic and Paralympic games, France had a record year for French tourism, bringing in over 100 million international tourist arrivals in 2024, according to Campus France.





Spain:



Spain also had a record year for tourism, bringing in 94 million international tourists in 2024, according to AP News. This was Spain’s second record-breaking year since 2019. Study International reports Spain has plenty to offer historically, culturally and architecturally like Antoni Gaudi’s Sagrada Familia and Park Güell in Barcelona, the Alhambra in Granada or El Retiro Park in Madrid. The country has also gained popularity for international students studying abroad.


United States:

The United States has seen international visitor numbers increase over the past few years, rising from 66.3 million travelers in 2023 to 72.4 million in 2024.



The International Trade Administration reports that New York city was the largest “port of entry” with 6.1 million visitors, followed by Miami with 4.7 million visitors and Los Angeles with 3.4 million visitors. But according to USA Today, the U.S. is beginning to see a decrease in travel and booking cancellations. Concerns over President Trump’s tariffs, deportations, border arrests and his general rhetoric is turning travelers away from the country, influencing them to travel elsewhere. AP News reports that the National Travel and Tourism Office predicted in March that they expected international arrivals to increase by 6.5% to 77.1 million this year and exceed 2019 levels in 2026. But with growing concern about the U.S. from the rest of the world could derail this prediction, causing a delay pre-Covid levels to not be surpassed until 2029.

Other highly visited countries in 2024 include China, Italy, Turkey, Mexico, Thailand, Germany and the United Kingdom.


Social media influence on destination popularity

It may seem like each year there’s a new “hotspot” or one specific destination where everyone flocks to whether it be a European city or a tropical beach vacation. Where does this come from?

According to Statista, a study by American Express taken in Feb. 2023 among a sample of a little over 2,000 US travelers and 1,000 travelers from other countries around the world with a household income of at least $70k revealed that 75% of travelers are inspired to book their next trip based on what they’ve seen on social media.

Original data from Statista

Other factors of inspiration include pop-culture, seeing locations on TV, or trying different cuisine.
